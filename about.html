About Me

Industrial lab technician, emergency responder, and aspiring technologist building at the intersection of operations and innovation.

Background

Over a decade in industrial facilities, from coating process equipment at refineries to analytical tests in petrochemical labs. That path built a ground-level understanding of quality, safety, and operations that no classroom alone can replicate. Currently, I support polymer and glycol production at one of the Gulf Coast's largest integrated complexes, handling product release decisions, coordinating with cross-functional teams, and contributing to emergency response.

What I Bring

I combine technical lab skills with emergency response readiness and a technology focus. NREMT, FAA Part 107 remote pilot, and lab point of contact. I operate across domains that don't usually overlap. I've supported EHS drone operations, served as a turnaround safety training advisor, and act as a bridge between operations, engineering, and business stakeholders. I lead without a formal title through reliability, coordination, and follow-through.

Where I'm Headed

I'm pursuing a BS in Computer Science through WGU, focusing on programming, AI/ML, and data systems. My goal is to apply those skills to industrial technology integration: predictive quality analytics, digital twins, intelligent automation. I believe the most effective solutions come from people who understand both the technology and the operational reality it needs to serve.

Leadership Philosophy

I believe leadership is demonstrated through action, not titles.

Reliability on the Line

Being the person others rely on during night shift. When things go sideways at 2 a.m., the team needs someone who stays steady, communicates clearly, and follows through without being asked.

Voluntary Ownership

Volunteering for responsibilities beyond the job description. Coordinating sample priorities as lab POC, coaching peers on safety during turnarounds, and stepping up when supervision is needed, not because someone assigned it, but because the work matters.

Cross-Team Communication

Communicating clearly across teams with different priorities and vocabularies. Whether it's translating lab results for operations, aligning with engineering on quality holds, or briefing business stakeholders on product status, effective leadership means making sure the right information reaches the right people.
